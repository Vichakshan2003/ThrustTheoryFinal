History of Rocketry Timeline

Explore the milestones that shaped humanity's journey into space, from ancient experiments to interstellar ambitions.

1232 AD - Fire Arrows

Chinese inventors use gunpowder-filled tubes attached to arrows, marking one of the earliest uses of rocket technology.

1687 - Newton's Third Law

Isaac Newton publishes his Third Law — the foundation of rocket propulsion.

1903 - Tsiolkovsky's Rocket Equation

Konstantin Tsiolkovsky mathematically proves that rockets can work in the vacuum of space.

1926 - First Liquid-Fueled Rocket

Robert Goddard launches the first successful liquid-fueled rocket in Massachusetts.

1942 - V-2 Rocket Launch

Germany's V-2 rocket becomes the first long-range ballistic missile and touches the edge of space.

1957 - Sputnik 1

The Soviet Union launches Sputnik 1 — humanity’s first artificial satellite.

1969 - Apollo 11

Neil Armstrong and Buzz Aldrin walk on the Moon, powered by the Saturn V rocket.

1981 - Space Shuttle Columbia

NASA's reusable space shuttle program begins with Columbia's launch into orbit.

1998 - International Space Station

The ISS project begins, creating humanity's first permanent home in orbit.

2020 - SpaceX Crew Dragon

SpaceX's Crew Dragon becomes the first commercial spacecraft to carry astronauts to the ISS.
