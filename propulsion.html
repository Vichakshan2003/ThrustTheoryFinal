Understanding Rocket Propulsion

Propulsion is what makes a rocket a rocket. It’s the science of getting off the ground — literally. From ancient gunpowder tubes to electric ion thrusters, propulsion systems have evolved into some of the most powerful machines humans have ever built. Let’s explore how we harness controlled explosions to conquer the skies.

Solid Fuel

Simple and reliable. Once lit, they burn until depletion. Used in boosters like the Space Shuttle SRBs.

Learn More

Liquid Fuel

Controlled, adjustable thrust. Used in Saturn V and Falcon 9. Burns fuel and oxidizer stored separately.

Learn More

Hypergolic

Ignite on contact — no spark needed. Popular for spacecraft maneuvering systems like Apollo LEM.

Learn More

Hybrid Engines

Solid fuel + liquid oxidizer. Combines features of both systems, seen in SpaceShipOne.

Learn More

Ion Propulsion

Used in deep space missions like Dawn. Accelerates ions to produce thrust with extreme efficiency.

Learn More

Nuclear Thermal

Still experimental. Uses a nuclear reactor to superheat hydrogen. Extremely high efficiency.

Learn More

Electric Propulsion

Solar-powered. Works best in space. Ideal for long-term satellite operations and interplanetary travel.

Learn More

Solar Sails

Propelled by photons. No fuel required. Pure physics meets science fiction — and now, reality.

Learn More
